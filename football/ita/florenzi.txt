Alessandro Florenzi (Italian pronunciation: [ales'sandro flo'r?ntsi]; born 11 March 1991) is an Italian professional footballer who plays as a midfielder or full-back for Roma and the Italy national team. Florenzi has been appointed as Roma captain following the departure of Daniele De Rossi .[2]

Florenzi started his professional club career with Roma in 2011, and has remained with the side ever since, aside from a loan spell with Crotone during the 2011–12 season. At international level he was a member of the Italy national under-21 football team that finished second in the 2013 UEFA European Under-21 Championship, and also represented the Italy senior side at UEFA Euro 2016.

On 14 November 2012, Florenzi made his debut for the senior team under coach Cesare Prandelli in a friendly match against France.[16]

Florenzi was part of the Italy under-21 squad for the 2013 UEFA European Under-21 Championship in Israel, as the Azzurrini reached the final, losing to Spain 4–2.[17] Florenzi's only goal of the tournament came in the 4–0, group stage, victory over the host nation.[18]

On 15 October 2013, Florenzi scored his first senior goal on his competitive debut in a World Cup qualifying match against Armenia that ended in a 2–2 draw at the Stadio San Paolo in Naples.[19] Florenzi was not included in the 30-man preliminary squad named by Cesare Prandelli for the 2014 FIFA World Cup.[20]

On 30 August 2014, new Italy head coach Antonio Conte named Florenzi in his first squad for the upcoming European qualifying matches.[21][22] On 31 May 2016, he was named to Conte's 23-man Italy squad for UEFA Euro 2016.
