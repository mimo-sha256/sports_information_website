Frenkie de Jong (born 12 May 1997) is a Dutch professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for Spanish club Barcelona and the Netherlands national team.

De Jong began his professional career at Willem II in 2015 and transferred to Ajax for a fee of €1 a year later. In the Dutch capital, starring as a defensive midfielder, he established himself as one of the best young players in Europe following his performances in the 2018–19 UEFA Champions League. With Ajax, he won the 2018–19 Eredivisie and was named the Eredivisie Player of the Season. He signed for Spanish club Barcelona in July 2019, having agreed to sign for them in a deal worth an initial €75m fee in January. De Jong was named to the 2019 FIFA FIFPro World11.

De Jong made his senior international debut for the Netherlands in September 2018 and starred for them in their run to the 2018–19 UEFA Nations League Final.

During his youth career, de Jong made 22 appearances for the various Netherlands youth teams. On 10 July 2015, he made his international debut for the Netherlands U19 team against Germany U19.[20] On 6 September 2018, de Jong made his senior international debut in a friendly 2–1 win against Peru.[21] He quickly became a regular starter for the Netherlands, featuring in almost every game of the 2018–19 UEFA Nations League and the UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying.
